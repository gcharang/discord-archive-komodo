I was just trying to figure out if I actually have an urge for some rum, or if it is just this blockchain. (edited)
I was sitting here having some thoughts about pirate. I keep seeing people in other sites shill pirate chain by pointing out that 99% of the unspent transactions are shielded. That always sounds strange to me. I mean, people can say whatever they feel, but it just seems irrelevant (mostly just miners acting according to the parameters). I would think it would be more accurate, or at least more meaningful, to suggest "Pirate is a blockchain with required, zero-knowledge, anonymous transactions". I would assume it would be more clear to somebody hearing about pirate for the first time. The requirement to shield the transactions creates the largest anonymity set, in that analysis can not be performed on various data-points found in other anonymity methods depolyed by other coins. In addition, true anonymous transactions (actually no identifying information) are different than encrypted transactions. (edited)
I have seen the transparent transaction chain rules come up a few times, but the concern is misdirected. The chain rules require coinbase rewards to be transparent, which increases the strength of the network's security in having an accountability of newly generated pirate. If the coinbase is never spent, no data-points are created to be tracked. If it is ever sent, it is required to be zero-knowledge. There are no transaparent, and more importantly, no encrypted balance data to analyze.
The other transparent transaction rules are a key function of the first notaries to exist on a anonymous blockchain which are a main component in one of cryptocurrencies strongest 51% attack defenses. IMO, having special utility transaction types that function transparently and does not weaken the anonymity set is just a none point of contention. Seems to only come up when we discuss the 99% thing as it raises the unfounded questions.
Anyway, just my two pieces of eight.
