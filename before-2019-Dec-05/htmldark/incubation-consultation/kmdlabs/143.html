It appears that they are placing active RFID's on each fish caught. While this is OK, it probably is not necessary. All we really need is location information on the fishing vessel which is a GPS signal. The skipper can issue a blanket barcode or QR code for the complete haul. Sensors will transmit temperature and other data while on the fishing vessel. Then at port the fish will be processed and packed. At port individual package codes are generated at the palleting stage and uploaded to the blockchain. Tracking continues for onward transportation by associating the smaller package Bar/QR Codes with the pallets (or equal) they are riding on or in until final delivery to the retail store. (This is in the case of fish, similar sequences apply for other products). We can bribe the retail consumer with a few AXO's to scan and report their purchase or oonsumption of the fish with our smart phone "Report App" that signals to the Oracle that the supply chain is complete. When the retail store receives the shipment payment can be triggered via a number of possible events all of which are reported by the oracle to the blockchain. One possible event is crossing a "geofence" our user would set up on our platform. (The geofence comes to us via an API from our GPS and cell network signal partner. (edited)
