gcharang 17-Jun-19 04:23 PM
@wbradt this is the protocol:
A has BTC, B has KMD. They agreed on swap price `1 BTC = 100 KMD` A is the maker, B is the taker. 1) B pays the dex fee so that A will start the process by locking his coins. 2) A picks randomNumber: `secret_A` A creates a utxo on the Bitcoin blockchain with value: 1 BTC encumbrance: the value can be claimed by A after 20 hours or by B if he knows the secret `secret_A` 3) After seeing the above utxo, B creates the following utxo on the KMD blockchain: value: 100 KMD encumbrance: the value can be claimed by B after 10 hours or by A if he knows the secret `secret_A` 4) When A takes the KMD, he reveals the secret `secret_A` which will allow B to claim BTC The times can be adjusted based on requirements of security and "ease of use" Normally, A should have a longer locktime so that B can have sufficient time to get his coins back if A don't reveal the secret `secret_A`
Ofcourse, once the order is matched and the swap starts, you will only be dealing with one other person
