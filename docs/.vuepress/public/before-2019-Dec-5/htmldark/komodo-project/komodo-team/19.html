Regnar 09-Jul-18 11:59 PM
The difference is many of these entities that will be providing BaaS to businesses can use any platform including Komodo. There are businesses out there that want to use Komodo but are hesitant to work with a decentralized group of anonymous developers. They could use a 3rd party but we would be leaving a huge hole in the market of established businesses looking to partner with a coin team. A separate but official entity provides us some means of outreach to businesses that shows we are a legitimate project and with close ties with the developing team. This isn't saying all businesses are going to go through this official team and not a 3rd party, but right now Komodo is in a spot where they have no way of reaching out in any official capacity. I think having an official entity will help us bridge that gap. (edited)
having the entity technically separate prevents any legal recourse from happening to the entity from Komodo actions or refusal of KYC, and any recourse happening to the business from shutting down the Komodo project. The "official" capacity should be in the closeness of the relationship, and the use of the entities revenues to hire/pay Komodo devs for their work and redistribute the remaining in a way that helps grow the ecosystem.
