neutrality is all the more important because blockchain is the area of communication that even platforms need to be compatible. Google and Facebook will never participate in blockchain that was created by Kakao or Line. on the other hand, TCP/IP, the Internet protocol, is free and voluntary. this is because the protocol itself is neutral and there is no need to rely on a particular principal. In other words, in order for blockchain to become a protocol, preconditions such as fixity, firmness, market competitiveness, neutrality, and compatibility must be met. Protocols should be fixed. While Bitcoin appears and developers who have jumped into the industry may be young Silicon Valley-type developers, it seems that they think change and improvement are ideal and good directions. Ethereum has been redirected or modified once every six months, and various blockchaines have sought to apply new technologies to suit the trend each time. What they did not understand was that for the ecosystem to develop, protocols must be stable and unchanged. TCP/IP has rarely changed since its advent. This fixedness and stability give businesses and developers confidence to create the protocol-based service. If entrepreneurs are creating something and some developers have to arbitrarily modify protocols to make them incompatible or change the direction of the business, who will inject capital and time?
1
Thus, diversification/multi-diversification of the technological development of an industrial group is not an absolute development Building technology and services on fixed protocols is a real development. For bitcoinSV, the components of the protocol are not changed, while ensuring scalability. If a change in BTC, such as segwit, RBFs, etc., or unverified technologies such as CTOR and Pre-consensus in BCH are simply started to be preferred and good by a particular developer, the change will be sustainable and impedes the development of the ecosystem. Bitcoin SV launched 14 protocol layers, 35 development tools, and about 161 services in the eight months since its inception. It's like 0.7 projects a day were born every day. The reason why this explosive growth can be achieved is that the protocol is guaranteed to be secure and scalable. Unfortunately, the worst misunderstanding created by the emergence of Vitalik and Ethereum is the trilemma. Scalability, de-centrality, and security mean that three rabbits cannot be caught at once. For example, if you want to gain the scalability and security of a blockchain, you have to give up decentralization. Thanks to the trilemma, the blockchain industry is divided into blockchain that emphasizes "remoteness plus security" and blockchain that emphasizes "extended plus security."
1
A representative example of blockchain that emphasizes decentralization and security is bitcoin. They have practically given up on expansion and are obsessed with digital gold and safety assets, which are economically insane ideologies. On the other hand, examples of blockchain emphasizing scalability and security are EOS or Kakao's Clayton-type blockchain, where participation in the network is not competitive author denies the trilemma itself. reason why trilemma does not exist is that of these three factors, scalability, decentrality, and security, the definition of de-centrality itself is wrong Decentralization never means the number of nodes. Just because the number of nodes in a particular blockchain is larger than the other blockchain does not mean that it is more de-centralized. The word decentralization should be replaced by the competitive openness of the network.
1
Decentralization is essentially the robustness of the network. this means that the network cannot be interrupted by a particular entity, whether it is a hacker or a state. Blockchain developers seem naive to think that as many nodes as possible should have all the data for the robustness of the network. What they didn't understand was that the robustness of the network was not affected by the number of nodes, but by the connections between the nodes. centrality and centralization of network topology are distinctly different concepts. Bitcoin evolves over time into a network that is centrality. As few hubs as possible when the miners are connected and the transactions are spread over the entire network, so that they can be safely transferred even if they are not a confirm.
1
In order for the network to go through scalability, security, and de-centralization, the BTC's 'Raspberry Pi Node' based network is in vain because the nodes participating in the network must be connected together. When nodes distributed around the world are not directly connected to each other, and are connected only to the surrounding nodes, the network itself is only configured in mesh form, and this type of network is never safe nor expandable. After all, powerful nodes that can connect to each other have to be responsible for the security and expansion of the network. Bitcoin's original design was for large data centers to form networks. And the reason why those data centers can be created is "economy." Blockchain's focus on security plus scalability described above is a very important missing element. All protocols, as well as blockchain, expand on the basis of economics. The reason the Internet Protocol, which was not able to send a picture, was able to communicate and stream 5G is because of the economy that provided an incentive to advance the technology.
