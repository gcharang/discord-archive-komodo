[dscrdvrsc] <Oink> I've got a nice discussion starter here. We already discussed it a bit in #off-topic , but I feel more people may have an opinion on the matter:

[16-May-19 10:37 PM] Gateway#3763
[dscrdvrsc] <Oink> With mining activities using enormous amounts of electricity from the mentioned Common-Pool Resources (CPR) and that amount ever growing, that would mean that PoW blockchains or even our own PoP mechanism can in the end not be sustained.

Mining is meant as a mechanism to make the chain work, secure it. An incentive to help the chain is offered in the form of a block-reward. Nowadays the majority of the miners don‚Äôt mine to secure a network, but their sole purpose is cold, hard profit.

While mining is profitable, hardware developers produce more powerful miners, that use more energy. To feed these beasts, the need to be centralized in areas where energy is cheap. In order to stay making profits, the need exists to keep buying that equipment, which raises the profits to your old level. The only ones making more profits are the energy companies and the hardware manufacturers.

The undesired result for any blockchain is that mining power is heavily centralized.

IMO the only way to keep sustaining PoW is to severely limit the maximum allowed hashrate on a chain. The biggest question is: how?

[16-May-19 10:37 PM] Gateway#3763
[dscrdvrsc] <Oink> Since mining is done locally, the chain has no influence over the amount of hashpower needed to solve a block, except for varying the difficulty. Since the difficulty is basically the governor for the chains blocktime and depends itself on the total networkhash, using network difficulty is not an option.

Out PoS system uses a minimum UTXO age to prevent from 1 UTXO a second time within 150 blocks. Could we use a similar mechanism to limit a miner from successfully submitting a block to the chain if he already successfully submitted one in the last, say 150 blocks?

If we could implement this mechanism and make the block submission cool down time dependent on a desired network hashrate. The
Desired network hashrate could be dialed in, in such a way that mining on a raspberry pi would give you the same chance to find a block, as an F1+ FPGA. Combine that with a block submission cool down time for new addresses and switching to a new address won't give you an advantage as a miner.

[16-May-19 10:37 PM] Gateway#3763
[dscrdvrsc] <Oink> Mechanisms to bypass this limitation would be possible, but implementing that the block submission cool down time only works when that wallet is still mining (at a minimum hashrate, a fraction of the power used, to create a heartbeat for the miner) then it would get increasingly difficult to bypass the system.

Such a mechanism would stimulate decentralization (pool mining will be obsolete), reduce global energy consumption, reduce the need to develop faster specialized mining hardware and make it about securing the network again, instead of profit.
