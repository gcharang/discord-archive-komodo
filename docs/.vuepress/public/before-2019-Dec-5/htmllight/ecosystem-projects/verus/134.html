[dscrdvrsc] <miketout> Interesting question. They're really not the same thing. though there could be some overlap in larger projects. In fact, each of the PBaaS chains are not exactly children, and are actually started with transactions that make offers to the entire Verus community of miners and Verus owners to participate in, mine, and support their projects and applications through a scalable, decentralized cross-chain protocol. This will enable even transiently created and abandoned blockchains to operate on behalf of applications, or long term projects to start up with easy configuration and a scalable future, without requiring any immediate development or alternate infrastructure provisioning on the part of the person, organization, or application creating the blockchain for its use. PBaaS chains could even be fairly launched, expected to stay coupled to Verus or another Verus chain as part of a project or application, or even launched and separated at a later date. They can also have pre-mines, funding/conversion periods, and will be able to earn their own native rewards, Verus, or a combination of the two. All chains will be automatically merge-mineable, have support for 51% hash attack protection, cross chain transactions, and all basic features of Verus, as well as the ability to develop smart transactions to support applications.
