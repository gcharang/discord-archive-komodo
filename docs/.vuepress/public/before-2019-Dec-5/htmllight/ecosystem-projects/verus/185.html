Miners and stakers will make blocks that can include conversions to and from a reserve currency. The reserve ratio is determined by a starting point, and then can be modified at every block by things like emission and burn transactions. The reserve ratio is then a function of the starting reserve and net emission. In any single block, all transactions, both buy and sell that can be processed, are, whether or not there are sellers when people want to buy or buyers when people want to sell. All conversions processed in a single block get exactly the same conversion price for both buys and sells (NO SPREAD!) There will be a small fee that effectively beats all exchanges but will go only to miners and stakers of the PBaaS reserve chains, which is where the conversions take place, providing even more of a network effect for merge mining and profit opportunities for people who mine and stake chains well beyond block emissions. [dscrdvrsc] <miketout> It's kind of like that, but it's also a little more complex. For example, the Bancor equations used for converting from one fractional currency to its reserve, which are simplified forms of our equations look like this:
to convert reserve to fractional: fractionalCurrencyOut = (supply * (pow((reservein / reserve + 1.0), ratio) - 1)) or to convert fractional to reserve: reserveCurrencyOut = reserve * (pow((fractionalin / supply + 1.0), (1 / ratio)) - 1)
These equations are solved together for all transactions in a block and all transactions in each block get the same price in either direction. That means that a 1 million Verus reserve could be used in a coin with a 10 million Verus market cap, and if people convert to the coin or use it more than convert from it, it will go up, if it is sold, the price will go down, but all coins, even to the last coin can be sold for Verus through the equations. The depth of the order book is always the entire money supply on the sell side and reserve on the buy side. (edited)
[dscrdvrsc] <miketout> When fractional reserve coins are emitted, the reserve ratio goes down. When they are officially burned, the reserve ratio goes up.
[dscrdvrsc] <miketout> the closer the reserve ratio is to 1:1, the less volatile the currency can be relative to Verus. the further from it, the more volatile and subject to market forces.
[dscrdvrsc] <miketout> you will be able to send Verus to reserve chains and to an address on that chain. As part of that send, you will be able to auto convert it to the native PBaaS currency or leave it as Verus tokens on the PBaaS chain and convert it when you want. You will be able to send Verus tokens without gas, because you can pay fees in Verus, which will convert automatically to the native currency. You can also convert any PBaaS reserve currency to Verus with these formulas and send it back to the Verus chain as well.
