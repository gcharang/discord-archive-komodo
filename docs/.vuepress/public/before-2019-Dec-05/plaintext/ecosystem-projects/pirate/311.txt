[14-Nov-18 04:23 AM] NutellaLicka#4241
@wolfgang It depends what you are looking for in a pool. 

The things I would look for in a pool are the following:
Minimum payout (in ARRR): Does my hash rate mean I need a higher/lower payout so I am getting regular payouts, rather than waiting a day or so to be paid.
Pool fee %: What do they do with that %, do they contribute a % to the project or contribute personally? Am I happy to pay that % for the quality of service they offer.
The website design and layout: Is it is an eyesore or does it appeal to me personally? Is it easy to use? Does it provide me with the information I require easily? 
Pool stability: Is the pool reliable? Do the offer backup ports to set my miner too? Have they had a period of downtime lately, has this been rectified? Are they proactive with informing everyone about the reasons why?
Pool features: Does the pool offer any special features you would be interested in?

And finally, hash rate:
For new miners, the other thing I would educate myself on is pool hash rates. It is important to know that a high hash rate pool means that you will receive smaller, more frequent payouts. Compared to a low hash rate pool that will provide higher payouts, at a lower frequency. Over time, theoretically they will both average out to the same. A higher hash rate pool will provide payouts closer to the average, a lower hash rate pool could provide payouts outside the average on a day to day basis (both higher and lower).

These are just my thoughts when coming to choosing a pool, I'd be happy to take feedback.
